What We're Losing

🌊

The Ocean's Heartbeat Is Slowing

Coral spawning — the largest synchronized reproduction event on Earth — is triggered by moonlight timing. Millions of corals release eggs and sperm on the same night, guided by lunar cycles.

Intertidal zones, among the most biodiverse ecosystems on the planet, depend on precise tidal ranges. Species have evolved for specific water levels at specific times.

As the Moon recedes, tides weaken. Nutrient mixing slows. The signal that synchronizes ocean life is fading.

"Moonlight is the trigger for the largest synchronised spawning event on the planet. Change the signal, and the entire reproductive cycle of reef ecosystems is at risk."

— PNAS, Studies on Coral Reproductive Synchrony
⏰

Our Infrastructure Is Already Failing

Earth's rotation is slowing by 2.3 milliseconds per century. That doesn't sound like much — until you realize our entire technological civilization depends on precise timing.

Since 1972, we've added 27 leap seconds to keep atomic clocks synchronized with Earth's actual rotation. GPS, financial markets, power grids, air traffic control — all require constant correction.

In 2035, leap seconds will be abolished. Not because the problem is solved — because the corrections have become too disruptive to manage.

🔄

Mars Is Our Preview

Earth's axial tilt — the reason we have seasons — is stabilized at 23.4° by the Moon's gravitational influence. Without it, our axis would wobble chaotically.

Mars has no large moon. Its axial tilt swings between 0° and 60° over millions of years. The result: extreme climate chaos that may have stripped the planet of its atmosphere and any chance of life.

As the Moon retreats, its stabilizing grip weakens. Earth's tilt could eventually swing from 0° to 85°. Summers at the poles. Ice ages at the equator. A world unrecognizable.

"The Moon is the principal reason that Earth's spin axis precesses at a nearly constant obliquity. Without it, the obliquity would undergo large chaotic variations over timescales of tens of millions of years."

— Jacques Laskar et al., Nature, 1993

The Threshold We Can't See

There's a resonance threshold — a distance at which the Moon's stabilizing effect breaks down and Earth's axial tilt becomes chaotic.

Scientists know it exists. They don't know exactly where it is.

We're approaching a cliff in the dark. And we've only been measuring for 55 years.

We're Flying Blind

Of the ~40 satellite laser ranging stations worldwide, only about 5 have lasers powerful enough to reach the Moon.

In 2009, McDonald Observatory — which had been tracking the Moon since 1969 — lost its $125,000 annual funding. The NSF deemed it redundant.

The International Laser Ranging Service doesn't fund stations. They rely on "host nation" budgets — whatever each country decides to allocate.

55 years of data for a 4.5 billion year process

We have better trajectory data for spacecraft leaving the solar system than for the Moon that controls our climate stability.

"Lunar laser ranging is one of the most precise tests of gravitational physics we have. The fact that we're losing stations is not a scientific decision — it's a funding failure."

— Dr. Tom Murphy, UC San Diego, Apollo Lunar Ranging Experiment (APOLLO)

The Departure Schedule

4.5 billion years ago

The Moon forms just 22,500 km away — 17× closer than today. It fills the sky. Days are 5 hours long. Tides are measured in kilometers.

620 million years ago

Days are 22 hours. Complex life is just emerging in the oceans. The Moon is already leaving — and life is evolving to its rhythms.

Now

384,400 km away. 24-hour days. Leap seconds failing. Coral spawning destabilizing. You are here, watching it leave.

+50 million years

The Moon is ~2,000 km farther. Tidal ranges noticeably weaker. Coastal ecosystems that couldn't adapt have collapsed.

+600 million years

Last total solar eclipse. The Moon can no longer fully cover the Sun. Humanity's most awe-inspiring celestial event ends forever.

+1–2 billion years

Earth's axial tilt destabilizes. Seasons become extreme and unpredictable. The climate chaos that sterilized Mars begins here.
